# Voice Fingerprint (example — replace with your own)

`/short-form-caption` reads this file to write captions in YOUR voice instead of generic AI voice. Fill every section from your own content (pull 20–40 of your best captions, video transcripts, or posts and look for the patterns). The richer this file, the less the output sounds like a robot.

## 1. Voice summary
Two or three sentences describing how you sound. Example: "Direct, builder-energy. Contrast-driven: name the broken way, then the fix. Talks to one person, not an audience. No hype words."

## 2. Sentence rhythm
- Typical sentence length (short fragments? medium? mix?)
- Do you use one-line paragraphs? Lists? Dashes?
- Punctuation habits (you may forbid em dashes here, etc.)

## 3. Vocabulary
- Words you use a lot.
- Words you NEVER use (corporate filler, hype, etc.).

## 4. Signature phrases
Five to ten phrases that are unmistakably yours. The caption command deploys 2–3 per caption, never all of them. Examples: "That's the unlock.", "fam", "Here's the move:".

## 5. Point of view
First person? Second person? How direct? How much do you swear / joke / get technical?

## 6. The pivot
How you turn from problem to solution. Example: "So flip it." / "The fix:" / "Here's what actually works:"

## 7. Empty patterns to avoid
The AI-slop phrasings you never want to see in your captions. Examples: "In today's world", "Let's dive in", "Most people use X wrong", "Here's what nobody tells you".

## 8. Diagnostic checklist
A few yes/no questions to run on a draft before shipping. Example:
- [ ] Does it sound like ME, not a brand account?
- [ ] Is there a specific pain in the hook, not a generic one?
- [ ] Did I avoid every phrase in section 7?
